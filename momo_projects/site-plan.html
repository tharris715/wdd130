Overview

Purpose

The purpose of the website is to introduce people to crochet and cross-stitch and the projects you can make using these methods, featuring my wife's creations. It will display her projects as well as go into the techniques used for each project.

Audience

The intended audience are those who ask about her projects. This website will give her an easy way to pull up examples of her work and how she made them.

Home page

Background Momo has enjoyed cross-stitching for XX years. She learned to crochet in her youth, but started expanding her skills over the past few years. Crochet is a good way to relax at the end of a long day of working in a research lab and raising a 2 year old. Purpose/Introduction This website will show off the crochet and cross-stitch projects created by Momo Harris. Here you can get an idea of the kinds of projects that can be created and see my level of skill. Please enjoy these projects as they have brought joy to me those I have gifted them to. Feel free to contact me about any questions that may arise as you browse my collection. Almost all of these projects were made using patterns found online. To access the pattern, click the image of my version to be directed to the website the pattern originated from. Some projects were modified from the original patterns.

Easy Projects

On this page you will see some of the patterns that will be easier for beginners. Some of these projects can be completed in a couple of hours but the product is still cute. If you want to make something today, you could try the ice cream cone rattle or the small mice. I made many of the ice cream cones for friends' baby showers and they always went over well. My work involves research with mice, hence my interest in making mice, and coworkers and friends would ask for them as well. Techniques These projects use more basic stitches. These would be better choices for those newer to crochet. However they can still pose a challenge, especially with the smaller pieces, which need small and tight stitches, which can make it tricky to move the hook through the stitches without breaking the yarn. You should test the tensile strength of the yarn so you know if you can pull on it hard enough to make the small stitches. Stitches and techniques used for these projects include the single crochet stitch, the most basic stitch in crochet. The half double crochet stitch which is slightly taller than the single crochet stitch. As well as the the double crochet stitch and treble crochet stitch which are both taller still.

Challenging Projects

Overview Displayed here are the more difficult pieces of work. These take longer to make or take a little more skill that the projects on the easy page. The projects that take the most time by far are the cross-stitch pieces. The Pokemon cross-stitch was the most ambitious project I have taken on. This represents about 3 years of working on it after college classes or work. It was finished around the same time my son was born and now hangs in his room. Techniques Some of the patterns in these projects may be a bit more difficult, including the phoenix and dragon. These took more effort than many of the other projects, because of the detail involved. These projects will take longer, and so you should plan ahead if you wish to make them. Stitches and techniques used for these projects includeThe shell stitch which uses a series of double crochets into the same stitch to create a shell-like pattern. The popcorn stitch which involves making a cluster of stitches in the same stitch to create a raised texture. However, these projects also use many of the easier stitches as well, it's just a matter of how you use stitches that make a project look more complicated.
